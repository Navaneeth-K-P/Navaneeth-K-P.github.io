A very different experience

Having missed out on my last research internship due to covid, I was very persistant on making sure that I make up for that loss. This was very crucial now that I was 100% sure that research and higher studies is gonna be my first priority. Throughout the academic year, I pushed myself to learn as much as I could and also complete most of my degree by the end of third year. I was succeful in doing so and is left with only one core course and a specialization project to complete my dgree and specialization in robotics. Now that I was almost done with that, the next goal was to work on good projects. DSL810 and the MasterCard design challenge turned out to be two great projects which I am really proud of but it was not related to Robotics.


With all this being the situation, and also the pandemic not showing any signs of vanishing, I decided to try for an online internship so that I could do some really good work and also prep for GRE in the comfort of my home. I had been looking out for proffessors working on domains I fould fascinating. However, there was one team that had my attention since last year, Marmot Lab (NUS) . The lab focused on Multi-Agent robotics which was one of the domains I was looking forward to explore. I had heard a lot about them from Priyanshi who interned with them last summer. I ended up contacting Prof.Guillaume who responded positively and welcomed me onboard. The meetings with the team was very much different from what I had expected. Just like Jay sir, he was also keen on his students coming up with their own research ideas and projects. The meetings also had different dynamics that what I was used to at IIT. Here, it was similar to a chat between a bunch of colleagues, a very stress-free (rarely seen in any meeting I attend in my college) but at the same time, very productive environment. Since I had chosen to work on Multi-agent search (over hexapod robots which would have been more involved with what I learned in JRL302 and MCL745 and more kinematics intensive), I had limited knowledge about what was being discussed but with the help of google on the side I was able to figure out a good part of it. Even though some of my friends were confused about my choice of MA search over Hexapod, I was very much interested in learning the new domain and the resources provided by Prof. was very helpfull in catching the basics.

However, after discussions with the team, I realized that it was not as connected to what was MA Search as I thought it was. However, I found another idea that came up in the meeting quite fascnating. It was linked to multi-agents with some form of consumable resource in it.
